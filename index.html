Overview

Seventeen (Korean: ÏÑ∏Î∏êÌã¥; stylized in all caps or as SVT) is a South Korean boy band formed by Pledis Entertainment. The group consists of thirteen members: S.Coups, Jeonghan, Joshua, Jun, Hoshi, Wonwoo, Woozi, DK, Mingyu, The8, Seungkwan, Vernon, and Dino. Throughout their career, they have reached a large audience and grown into an internationally recognized K-pop group known for signature music and performances.

Debut & Major Records

The group debuted on May 26, 2015, with the EP 17 Carat, which became the longest-charting K-pop album of the year in the US and the only rookie album included in Billboard's "10 Best K-Pop Albums of 2015". Their 2023 EPs FML and Seventeenth Heaven set sales records; FML brought their first MAMA Album of the Year and became one of the year's best-selling albums worldwide.

Self-Producing Identity

Seventeen is widely regarded as a "self-producing" idol group ‚Äî members contribute to songwriting, music production, and choreography. They are divided into three specialized units: hip-hop, vocal, and performance, while also promoting as a full group. Their stage work and synchronization have earned them nicknames such as "Performance Kings" and "Kings of Synchronization."

Name & Fanclub

The name "Seventeen" comes from the idea "13 members + 3 teams + 1 group". Their official fanclub name is Carat, announced on February 14, 2016 ‚Äî a nod to their debut EP 17 Carat and the song "Shining Diamond".

Sub-units & Solo Work

Notable sub-units include BSS (BooSeokSoon), JxW, and the duo Hoshi X Woozi. Members also participate in solo projects and the ongoing "Thirteen Tapes" releases.

Artistry & Choreography

Seventeen's musical identity is shaped by heavy member involvement in writing and producing (notably Woozi) and elaborate choreographies often co-produced by Hoshi. Their lyrics frequently touch on relatable themes like growth, perseverance, and comfort.

Other Ventures & Endorsements

Seventeen have collaborated with global brands (Apple, Cigna, Lazada, Bibigo, etc.), acted as ambassadors, and appeared in major campaigns and endorsements across Asia and internationally.

Philanthropy & UNESCO

The group regularly donates to charitable causes and partners with UNESCO for education initiatives. In 2023 they were named UNESCO Goodwill Ambassadors for Youth and pledged support for global youth projects.

Awards & Recognition

Multiple domestic and international awards, including MAMA Album of the Year (for 2023 & 2024 albums), Prime Minister's Commendation, MTV recognitions, and many year-end Bonsang/Daesang awards.
